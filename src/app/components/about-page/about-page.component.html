About ChantLab

ChantLab is an advanced tool designed to assist researchers, musicologists, and enthusiasts in the analysis and comparison of Gregorian chant melodies. The platform provides a robust set of features for exploring, aligning, filtering, and visualizing chant melodies, making it an invaluable resource for the study of plainchant transmission across centuries and regions.

Key Features

  • Melody alignment and comparison across chant sequences.
  • Visualization tools for exploring melodic similarities and variations.
  • Advanced filtering options to focus on specific parts of the chant data.
  • Alignment of melodies from different sources using identical Cantus IDs.
  • Concatenation of melodies from the same source, allowing for streamlined analysis.

Project Origins

ChantLab started as a bachelor thesis by Kristína Szabová. Her work, titled "Analytical Tools for Gregorian Chant" (2021), laid the foundation for this platform. Read the thesis.

Today, ChantLab is being developed under the Digital Analysis of Chant Transmission (DACT). This project, funded by the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada, aims to advance the study of chant dissemination using computational tools.
